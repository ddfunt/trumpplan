What will you find here?

President elect Trump released a 100 day plan a few weeks before the November election detailing the actions he would take in his first 100 days of office. He has labeled this plan his contract with the American People.

Here I plan to evaluate this plan for several criteria: Does he have the power to enact the plan, will the plan actually work to fix the problem, and most importantly, is the plan the right thing for the people.

On Bias

There has been a good bit of rather painful talk lately about bias and media. While I mostly believe it is overblown, I would be remiss if I did not say that everyone is biased even the media.

Yes I voted Clinton, and was vehemently against Trump, so how can I fairly review his policies? As a scientist we are accustomed to taking multiple literature sources, often conflicting, and extracting relevant data and finding flaws in methods. Where there are little or no sound sources we then have to create and apply our own hypotheses. I apply this same thought process to how I look at Trump's policies. I review multiple sources that compare historical precedent and predictive theory to create a logical conclusion.

Will my outcome be biased? Yes, but I will try my best to be as close to the center as humanly possible.
